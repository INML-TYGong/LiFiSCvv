Live High-Fidelity Semantic Communication via Cross-modal Fusion for Volumetric Video

Overview of LiFiSCvv.

LiFiSC semantic communication framework represents a breakthrough paradigm for efficient data transmission in next-generation communication networks. However, SC technologies face significant challenges, including insufficient semantic feature representation, high communication latency, and sensitivity to channel noise.

To address these limitations, we propose a cross-modal fusion-based general semantic communication framework with strong generative semantic compression and high visual fidelity. We then extend it to LiFiSCvv, specifically designed to achieve 3D volumetric video transmission and reconstruction with photorealistic visual quality and an end-to-end live watching experience with acceptable latency.

The proposed LiFiSCvv system comprises three key components:

  1. Information redundancy reduction through lightweight video analysis and structure-from-motion techniques, decreasing reconstruction cost;
  2. Cross-modal fusion learning-driven codec mechanisms that enable efficient semantic representation and robust transmission against channel impairments;
  3. Feed-forward vision transformer for rapid volumetric video reconstruction and rendering.

Our method takes unposed video as input. Initially, we design a video frame selection module with geometric and semantic priors to filter out keyframes. These keyframes are then encoded, transmitted, and decoded in real-time by our LiFiSC system. Finally, our proposed fast reconstruction module reconstructs point clouds from the received frames for immersive exploration.

Comprehensive evaluation results demonstrate that LiFiSCvv achieves an end-to-end real-time high-resolution photorealistic watching experience in seconds with 42× semantic compression, significantly outperforming other methods in general image transmission and volumetric video transmission.
